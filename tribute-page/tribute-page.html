Kobe Bryant

The Black Mamba

Timeline:

  • 1996 - Bryant is selected by the Charlotte Hornets as the 13th pick in the first round of the NBA draft and traded to the Los Angeles Lakers.
  • 1997 - Wins the All-Star Weekend slam dunk competition, but does not play in the All-Star game.
  • 1998 - Plays in first All-Star game and becomes the youngest starter ever at 19 years of age.
  • 2000 - Wins first NBA Championship with the Los Angeles Lakers.
  • 2001 - Wins second NBA Championship with the Los Angeles Lakers.
  • 2002 - Wins third NBA Championship with the Los Angeles Lakers.
  • 2002 - First of four times being named All-Star MVP. He's also named in 2007, 2009 and 2011.
  • 2005 - On December 20, Bryant scores 62 points in three quarters against the Dallas Mavericks.
  • 2006 - On January 22 2006, Bryant scores 81 points in the Lakers 122-104 win over Toronto, the second most points in a game in NBA history.
  • 2008 - Wins NBA regular season MVP award.
  • 2008 - Bryant wins a gold medal at the Beijing Olympics as a member of the US men's basketball team.
  • 2009 - Wins fourth NBA Championship with the Los Angeles Lakers with NBA Finals MVP honors.
  • 2010 - Wins fifth NBA Championship with the Los Angeles Lakers with NBA Finals MVP honors..
  • 2010 - He surpasses Jerry West's career record of 25,192 as the Los Angeles Lakers' all-time leading scorer, with 25,208 points.
  • 2012 - Is a member of gold-medal-winning US men's basketball team at the 2012 London Olympics.
  • 2013 - On April 12, tears his left Achilles tendon in game against Golden State Warriors.
  • 2013 - Bryant returns from his Achilles injury, but only plays 6 games due to injury.
  • 2014 - Bryant only plays 35 games in the 2014-15 season due to injury, but passes Michael Jordan on the NBA's all-time scoring list, with 32,310 career points.
  • 2015 - Makes his return in a preseason game against the Utah Jazz.
  • 2016 - On April 13, Scores 60 points in his final game, helping the Lakers defeat the Utah Jazz 101-96.

Honors and Awards:

  • 5× NBA champion (2000–2002, 2009, 2010)
  • 2× NBA Finals MVP (2009, 2010)
  • NBA Most Valuable Player (2008)
  • 18× NBA All-Star (1998, 2000–2016)
  • 4× NBA All-Star Game MVP (2002, 2007, 2009, 2011)
  • 11× All-NBA First Team (2002–2004, 2006–2013)
  • 2× All-NBA Second Team (2000, 2001)
  • 2× All-NBA Third Team (1999, 2005)
  • 9× NBA All-Defensive First Team (2000, 2003, 2004, 2006–2011)
  • 3× NBA All-Defensive Second Team (2001, 2002, 2012)
  • 2× NBA scoring champion (2006, 2007)
  • NBA Slam Dunk Contest champion (1997)
  • NBA All-Rookie Second Team (1997)
  • Naismith Prep Player of the Year (1996)
